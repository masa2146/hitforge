## draft-mutation-merge — "Draft-Mutation Merge"
- **status:** pending
- **signal:** ORIGINAL · **operator:** TRANSPLANT · **raw material:** `tile-merge-tycoon`
- **leap:** Between rounds you draft one of three board-wide mutations and permadeath wipes
  the economy — the merge board is rebuilt by roguelite runs, not a permanent grind.
- **mechanic:** You merge tiles for a few tense minutes, then pick one of three mutations
  that rewrite how the whole board behaves. A run ends in permadeath, resetting the economy
  so every session is a fresh build order. _(fingerprint: merge · draft · roguelite · run)_
- **similarity check:** not found (sample — a real run would search stores + ad libraries)
- **3-sec ad test:** a single mutation card flips the board from calm to chaos, numbers cascading.
- **scores:** video_clarity 4 · production_ease 3 · **novelty_risk 4** · meta_potential 4
- **why it might hit:** roguelite drafting gives merge fatigue a reason to keep starting over; short runs fit short sessions.
- **why it might not:** two loved systems can fight for attention; the merge crowd may resent losing progress to permadeath.

## be-the-parcel — "Be the Parcel"
- **status:** pending
- **signal:** ORIGINAL · **operator:** INVERT · **raw material:** `conveyor-sort-shooter`
- **leap:** You stop being the sorter and become the thing being sorted — ride the belt and
  steer yourself into your own colour gate while the machine fires at you.
- **mechanic:** You ride a conveyor as a coloured parcel, swiping to dodge a sorting arm that
  tries to fling you into the wrong chute. Reach your matching gate at the end of the line to
  score. _(fingerprint: conveyor · dodge · steer)_
- **similarity check:** partly exists — endless dodgers are common; the sort-your-own-colour twist is the untested part
- **3-sec ad test:** a near-miss as the arm swats and you slip into the correct gate.
- **scores:** video_clarity 5 · production_ease 4 · **novelty_risk 3** · meta_potential 3
- **why it might hit:** point-of-view inversion is instantly readable and screenshots like a chase.
- **why it might not:** dodgers are a crowded, low-retention space and the twist may be too thin to hold.

## tilt-crowd-slosh — "Tilt Crowd Sloshhh"
- **status:** pending
- **signal:** ORIGINAL · **operator:** CONSTRAINT-SWAP · **raw material:** `magnet-crowd-runner`
- **leap:** Control moves from finger taps to phone tilt, and the crowd behaves like liquid
  sloshing in a glass instead of a rigid blob.
- **mechanic:** You tilt the phone to steer a magnet, and the colour-matched crowd sloshes
  and spills with fluid physics as you swerve. Keep the most people in the glass to the
  finish gate. _(fingerprint: crowd · tilt · physics · magnet)_
- **similarity check:** partly exists — similar to `magnet-crowd-runner` (J≈0.33); tilt + fluid sloshing is the untested part
- **3-sec ad test:** a hard tilt and the crowd sloshes to one side, some spilling over the rim.
- **scores:** video_clarity 4 · production_ease 3 · **novelty_risk 4** · meta_potential 3
- **why it might hit:** fluid crowd physics is oddly satisfying and novel in ads; tilt is a fresh input.
- **why it might not:** tilt controls frustrate many players and hurt accessibility; physics is costly on low-end phones.

## spoilage-merge — "Spoilage"
- **status:** pending
- **signal:** ORIGINAL · **operator:** THEME-MECHANIC FUSION · **raw material:** `tile-merge-tycoon`
- **leap:** The fresh-produce theme is a rule, not a skin — every tile has a freshness timer
  and rots if unmerged, turning the economy into a race against spoilage.
- **mechanic:** You merge produce tiles that each carry a freshness timer, and any tile left
  too long rots and clogs the board. The economy becomes a constant pressure to merge before
  decay spreads. _(fingerprint: merge · decay · timer · pressure)_
- **similarity check:** partly exists — timed tiles appear in some puzzlers; decay-as-core-economy is the untested part
- **3-sec ad test:** a tile visibly rots and infects its neighbours as the player scrambles to merge.
- **scores:** video_clarity 3 · production_ease 4 · **novelty_risk 4** · meta_potential 4
- **why it might hit:** decay pressure fixes merge-idle's biggest flaw (no urgency); theme and mechanic reinforce each other.
- **why it might not:** constant time pressure can feel stressful and repel the cosy merge audience.

## zen-mailroom — "Zen Mail Room"
- **status:** pending
- **signal:** ORIGINAL · **operator:** AUDIENCE-SHIFT · **raw material:** `conveyor-sort-shooter`
- **leap:** Same sorting mechanic, re-cast for a relaxation audience: slow belts, no timer,
  no fail state, satisfying sort ASMR and cosy progression.
- **mechanic:** You calmly sort parcels onto matching shelves at your own pace, with soft
  tactile sounds and no clock. Progress unlocks a cosier, more personalised mail room over
  time. _(fingerprint: sort · cozy · zen · relax)_
- **similarity check:** partly exists — cosy/ASMR games are rising; this specific sort-relaxation framing is the untested part
- **3-sec ad test:** a single slow, satisfying parcel slide onto a shelf with a soft click.
- **scores:** video_clarity 3 · production_ease 5 · **novelty_risk 3** · meta_potential 4
- **why it might hit:** cosy/relaxation is a fast-growing, under-served segment with strong retention, and it is cheap to build.
- **why it might not:** relaxation games monetise slowly and are hard to make pop in a 3-second ad.
